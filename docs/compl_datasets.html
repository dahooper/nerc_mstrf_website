The following have been identified as being particularly useful to complement observations made by the NERC MST Radar - click on the title to go to the relevant page on the BADC.

Met Office high resolution radiosonde data
The data consist of vertical profiles of pressure, temperature, relative humidity, humidity mixing ratio, sonde position, wind speed and wind direction. Measurements are taken at 2 second intervals and the ascents extend to heights of approximately 20 - 30 km. Of particular relevance to the NERC MST radar observations are those radiosondes launched from Aberporth, approximately 45 km to the south-west of Aberystwyth.


Met Office Wind-Profilers
Data are available from the Met Office's boundary-layer wind-profilers located at Camborne, Dunkeswell and Wattisham and (formerly) Aberystwyth. The primary data products are 30 minute consensus average profiles of wind speed and direction. Radial velocities and signal-to-noise ratios for individual beam directions are also given. Observations are made in both a "low" mode, covering the approximate altitude range 0.1 - 2.0 km at 0.1 km intervals, and a "high" mode, covering the approximate altitude range 0.2 - 8.0 km at 0.2 km intervals; the useful coverage varies with atmospheric conditions.

Met Office Rain Radar (Nimrod)
Nimrod is a fully automated system for weather analysis and nowcasting based around a network of C-band rainfall radars. It provides fine-resolution analyses and six-hour forecasts. The BADC holds the analyses as a time resolution of 15 minutes. Nimrod delivers routine predictions of: Rainfall rate, Rain accumulation, Precipitation type, Snow probability, Cloud, Visibility and Wind gust speeds. Quick-look plots of the data are also available.
